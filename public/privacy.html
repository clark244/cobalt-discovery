Privacy Policy

Cobalt Collective — Impact Discovery tool · Last updated 22 July 2026 · Version 0.1 (interim)
Interim policy pending legal review. This is a working draft that describes, in good faith, how the Impact Discovery tool actually handles information today. It has not yet been reviewed by counsel and will be replaced by a final policy before or shortly after public launch. Questions: clark@cobaltcollective.org.

This policy explains what the Cobalt Collective Impact Discovery tool ("the tool") collects, how we use it, and your choices. Cobalt Collective is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. The tool is a free, advisory resource — it is not a funding application and using it is not a request for or promise of funding.

1. Information we collect

Identity you provideYour name (or initials) and email address, which you enter to begin. We email a one-time code to that address to confirm it is yours.
Your conversationThe full text of your discovery conversation with the tool, including everything you type about your organization, product, and plans, and the tool's responses and generated assessment.
Referral / traffic sourceHow you arrived at the tool — the referring web address and any campaign ("UTM") tags in the link you followed.
Optional follow-up emailIf you choose to leave an email at the end for a follow-up or updates, we store that separately.
Technical dataYour IP address and basic request information, used for security and to enforce usage limits.

Please do not enter information you are not authorized to share, and avoid submitting sensitive personal data (for example, health, financial, or identifying details about specific individuals). The tool is designed for describing your organization and product, not for personal records.

2. How we use it

  • To run the discovery conversation and generate your impact model, measurement plan, and summary.
  • To verify your email, prevent abuse, and enforce daily usage limits.
  • To understand how the tool is used and to improve it and Cobalt's services.
  • To follow up with you about Cobalt's work if you request it, or if you choose to start a conversation with us.

We do not sell your information, and we do not use it to make an automated funding or eligibility decision about you. A person at Cobalt reviews sessions.

3. AI processing

The conversation is processed by a third-party AI provider (Anthropic) to generate the tool's responses and your assessment. Your inputs are sent to that provider to deliver the service. We do not use your conversation to train foundation models.

4. Where your information is stored and who sees it

Session records — including your name, email, transcript, and generated assessment — are stored in Cobalt's Google Workspace (Google Sheets) and are accessible to authorized Cobalt staff. Cobalt staff may receive a notification when a session is completed. We use reputable service providers (including our website host and email provider) to operate the tool.

5. Retention

Interim approach, subject to change on legal review: we keep session records for as long as needed to operate and improve the tool and to follow up where appropriate. You may ask us to delete your information at any time (see below).

6. Your choices and rights

  • You can request a copy of, correction of, or deletion of your information by emailing clark@cobaltcollective.org.
  • You can decline to leave a follow-up email; the follow-up is optional.
  • Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights under laws such as the GDPR or CCPA. Contact us to exercise them.

7. Children

The tool is intended for adults (18+) using it in a professional or organizational capacity. It is not directed to children and should not be used to submit information about children.

8. Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your information, but no online service can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

9. Changes

We will update this policy as the tool evolves and once it has been reviewed by counsel. Material changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date above.

10. Contact
